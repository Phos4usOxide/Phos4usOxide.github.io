Film Making and Editing

Videos are the most effective way to show users/clients ideas, products, information, or entertainment. Creating high quality videos and films take a lot of planning, time, and resources. They also require a lot of complex steps, which can contain their own sub-steps. Let's check out some information and guidelines for film making and editing!

The 3 main stages of filming

Films are complex and require many steps. These steps are grouped together into 3 main stages.

  1. Pre-production: The planning needed to be done before the filming. Examples include script-writing and setting up a scene.
  2. Production: The actual filming including the the managment of cameras, lighting, and sounds.
  3. Post-production: Everything required after the filming, which is mainly editing. Examples include assembling all the clips and audio together and adding after-effects, filters, text, and CGI.

The 8 steps of filming

These steps are more specific than the 3 main stages since they contain important sub-steps for planning.

  1. Outline the idea.
  2. Write the script.
  3. Story-board each shot. This means deciding what each shot in the movie is going to be (e.g. wide shot, crane shot, close up, etc.).
  4. Formulate a breakdown sheet. This mans deciding what you need for each scene.
  5. Determine the setting of the scene, like whether it's external or internal.
  6. Determine what props, make-up, costumes, will be used. They must all make sense in each scene.
  7. Complete the principal photography. This refers to the actual filming with cameras, lights, and sound.
  8. Edit the film. Like mentioned before, this includes assembling clips and adding after-effects.

Lighting

The lighting when filming is a very important aspect that must be perfected.
